AUSPIBOR point 0.0.0. stems from AUSPIBOR legend

Boreas the north wind
Auster the south wind

Boreas always lived in north and Auster always lived in south

They were in love with each other. AUSPIBOR point was their date place.

AUSPIBOR point is a geographical point that is the junction between equator and zero meridian. Concretely speaking this point is off Ghana coast

Each AUSPIBOR model name is a geographical point.
For SPI SHIRT line the geographical points of each model design a Spi sail centered around the AUSPIBOR point 0.0.0

Ancient Book

This ancient book has been discovered by a member of the future AUSPIBOR Team in the wreck of a boat found at the bottom of the ocean.

In spite of the case protecting those writtings, this ancient book was in very bad shape. Nevertheless enough well preserved, the team opted to start restoring.

At the moment we are restoring the first tale.

The name AUSPIBOR has been inspired by this first legend telling the story of the encounter between Austral and Boreal.

To illustrate this first chapter, a SPI SHIRT line has been developed, inspired by Austral.

The book restauration being laborious, it will be impossible to release the full content in one shot.

It is the reason why, as far as possible, we are commited to offering for each tale restored a new AUSPIBOR collection.

It is a natural fiber made up of over 91% of cellulose. The cotton fiber can be spinned into carded or combed yarn

The continuous web stemming from carding process is combed out. Combing is all about separating out short fibers and getting parallel fibers. The fibers which result from the combing process are straightened and paralleled and shining.

carding is a process to clean and comb the cotton fibers. A continuous web is produced at the process end. This continuous web is soft and brilliant and bulky but with a low strength

The name «Pima» was applied to ELS cotton (previously called American-Egyptian) being developed in the U.S. desert southwest in the early 1900's. The name was given in honor of the Pima Indians who were helping to raise the ELS cotton on the USDA experimental farm in Sacaton, Arizona.

It takes a premium fiber to produce a luxury product. Pima growers know there are no shortcuts to quality. Often referred to as ‟the cashmere of cottons”, Pima surpasses other cottons in softness, strength, and brilliance of color

The staple length of Pima is 35% longer than regular cottons. This increases softness and luster. Fewer fiber ends are exposed, minimizing the effects of abrasion resulting in less pilling.

Pima is up to 45% stronger than regular cottons. This makes Pima products extraordinarily resilient. Even lighter-weight Pima fabrics are incredibly durable, without compromising drape and comfort.

Pima absorbs and retains color better than regular cottons. This means that Pima products retain their brilliance over many years of use.
